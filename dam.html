Mullaperiyar Gravity Dam

Civil Engineering & Structural Principles

This miniature model of the Mullaperiyar Gravity Dam demonstrates the fundamental principles of civil engineering. The project visually explains how hydrostatic pressure—the force exerted by standing water—interacts with large structures. It serves as a practical visualization of complex concepts, bringing textbook theories to life through a tangible design.

Focusing on structural stability, the model highlights how gravity dams utilize their own massive weight to resist the immense lateral pressure of water. Building this model required an understanding of scale, material mechanics, and the intricate balancing forces essential for the safe and robust design of major infrastructural frameworks.

Key Features:

  • Miniature model explaining principles
  • Hydrostatic pressure resistance
  • Structural stability of gravity dams
  • Basic civil engineering principles
